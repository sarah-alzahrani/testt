Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

The Mapping Metadata Vocabulary (MMV) is an OWL2 ontology that provides a structured, lifecycle-based model for representing metadata about mapping projects, mapping activities, and mapping artifacts. It supports the documentation of decisions and contextual information generated throughout the mapping lifecycle from analysis and design, to development, testing, and maintenance. MMV enables mapping practitioners, tool developers, and data publishers to capture metadata that improves transparency, reusability, and quality assessment of declarative mappings such as RML documents. The vocabulary reuses and aligns with well-established standards including PROV-O for provenance, DQV for data-quality assessment, and MQV for mapping quality metadata, ensuring interoperability and FAIR-compliant representation. MMV can be applied to document mapping workflows, support mapping verification and refinement, and enhance the long-term maintainability and reuse of mapping assets in data integration and knowledge graph generation projects.

Introduction back to ToC

The Mapping Metadata Vocabulary (MMV) was developed to address a common gap in the documentation and reuse of declarative mapping definitions used to generate knowledge graphs and Linked Data. While existing vocabularies such as PROV-O, DQV, and MQV provide foundations for expressing provenance, data quality, and mapping quality, there is no dedicated model that captures the full lifecycle context of mapping projects, how mappings are planned, designed, developed, tested, validated, and maintained over time. MMV introduces a lifecycle-oriented structure that models five key phases: Analysis, Design, Development, Testing, and Maintenance. Each phase contains activity classes and associated metadata fields that describe assumptions, motivations, decisions, tools used, requirements, risks, validation results, and other contextual factors influencing the mapping process. By providing explicit links between a Mapping Project, its Mapping Activities, and the resulting Mapping Artifacts, MMV enables richer documentation and traceability of mapping workflows. This is particularly important for complex data integration scenarios, collaborative environments, and long-term maintenance of mapping specifications.
